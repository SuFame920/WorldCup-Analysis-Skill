## Key Reasoning Chain

1. **Motivation asymmetry is real but nuanced.** Uruguay MUST win → maximum intensity. Spain: draw tops the group, loss still likely advances. BUT Spain has two counter-motivations: (a) avoid Argentina in R32 (H1 faces J2 = weaker; H2 faces J1 = Argentina), so winning the group matters; (b) build momentum — a quality win here sends a message. Net: Spain's motivation ~85-90% of Uruguay's, not 50%.

2. **Uruguay's defensive crisis vs Spain's attacking resurgence.** Uruguay missing Araujo (best defender) + De Arrascaeta (creative engine) is devastating. Conceded to Saudi Arabia (weakest attack in group) AND 2 to Cape Verde. Spain scored 4 against Saudi Arabia after figuring out how to break a block. Yamal + Nico Williams stretching wide against Uruguay's unsettled backline → mismatch.

3. **Uruguay's press is a double-edged sword vs Spain.** Bielsa's trademark high press + man-marking can disrupt Spain's build-up (Uruguay #1 in possession won in final third at Copa América). BUT it leaves space behind — exactly what Spain's pacy wingers exploit. Cape Verde (a 5-4-1 low block) held Spain 0-0 by sitting deep; Uruguay WON'T sit deep, they'll press high. This structurally opens the game more.

4. **Spain's clean sheet record is significant.** 0 goals conceded in 2 games. Saudi Arabia didn't score; Cape Verde didn't score. BTTS in only 3 of Spain's last 12 matches. Uruguay will test this more than previous opponents (set pieces + press-induced transitions), but Spain's defensive organization under De la Fuente is elite.

5. **H2H history: Uruguay have NEVER beaten Spain.** 10 meetings: 5 Spain wins, 5 draws. This isn't just statistical noise — Spain's possession style historically neutralizes Uruguay's physical approach. However, Bielsa's Uruguay is a different animal (higher press, more aggressive) than previous Uruguay iterations.

6. **The most likely game script:** Spain controls possession early, Uruguay press in bursts. Spain creates chances through wide areas (Yamal/Williams vs Uruguay's fullbacks). Uruguay threatens on set pieces. Spain scores first (30-45') → Uruguay has to chase harder → game opens up → Spain gets a second (55-70'). Uruguay may get one back from a set piece (75-85'). Final: 2-1 Spain.

7. **Calibration: MD3 subtype judgment.** This is closest to scenario ③ (出线缠斗) but with an asymmetry: Uruguay is in a must-win death match while Spain is in a "win or draw, fine either way" comfort zone. This is NOT scenario ⑤ (表演赛) — Uruguay has everything to play for. It's also NOT scenario ② (绞杀铁桶) — Uruguay won't park the bus. It's a hybrid: Uruguay's intensity + Spain's composure. The result is a game that trends toward Over (Uruguay opening up) but not wildly so (Spain's defensive structure + Uruguay's missing creative pieces).
