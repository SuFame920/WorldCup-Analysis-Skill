### Reflection (5-whys + channel attribution)

1. **Why was this the worst card of the batch?** Ecuador finally scored their first tournament goal AND a second, while Germany scored early but couldn't hold. Both predicted outcomes (Germany win + Under 2.5) were invalidated by Ecuador's offensive breakout.

2. **Why didn't we see Ecuador's offense coming?** They had 0 goals in 2 matches and 7 big chances missed — the narrative was "Ecuador can't score." But Sané's early goal (2') forced Ecuador out of their defensive shell, creating space that doesn't exist when they sit deep. The game-state mechanism (early concession → forced to attack → more open game) was underweighted vs season-level stats.

3. **Why was the "German win" conviction too high?** Nagelsmann's "only 2 changes" signal was overweighted — the team's intensity dropped after the early goal, suggesting motivational fatigue (already qualified). The early lead paradoxically hurt Germany: they had less incentive to push while Ecuador had max incentive.

4. **Why did the totals miss?** The "must-win + forced to attack after concession" dynamic wasn't priced in — both teams scoring early (2' + 9') opened up a game that was predicted to be a tight low-scoring affair.

5. **Root cause:** The pre-match profile labeled this as "Germany at near-full strength vs 0-goal Ecuador = low-scoring German win." But MD3 reversed the expected intensity vectors: Ecuador (must-win, desperate) played above their baseline while Germany (already qualified, nothing at stake) played below theirs. **The "MD3 liberation effect" applies not only to eliminated teams but also to must-win teams against already-qualified favorites.**

### Key transfer
- When a "must-win" team faces an "already-qualified" team, the must-win team's offensive output can be significantly above their tournament baseline (Ecuador: 0 goals in 2 games → 2 goals).
- Early goals in MD3 are a powerful game-opener: if the favorite scores early, the underdog abandons its defensive shell → more goals overall → upset potential increases.
- **Calibration update:** In MD3 asymmetric-motivation matches, the must-win team's expected goals should be bumped above their tournament average, especially if the opponent's key motivation (qualification was already secure) is weak.
